Man, I feel great. Coupla days from now is the birthday of my first some-type-of-love (not the first, well, the first woman I was totally in love with. She'd had enough by the miserable end of my first national tour, and was fucking a mutual friend on my birthday. Eventually she quit Mike iLL, and I ain't heard from her in years.

Shares Frank Sinatra's birthday, December 12th. She's the bomb for having the self-respect and disciplin to be silent.

Weather in Richmond is unseasonably warm, beautifull. The night sky out here in the suburbs is full of stars, and with a thin veil of cloud-spray. Puting me up are a couple who are an accountant and a soldier who's studying to be a nurse. So funny last night singing "Some will tel you I'll never be happy with the government of this country... Long as citizens starve while we spending our money on the military, Guess They're Right..". My man's in the front row, we're both looking at each other smiling and feeling a bit akward. Fact is, I respect a soldier (homeboy knows it), and respect a corporate leader - but not the wars they tend to wage.

Just like a "yuppie" is on his or her own path, but as a whole they seem to give nothing back to the community they gentrify.

Great Punk/Blues jam session the other night at Fat City in Charlotte. This cat Abe Reid runs it... Alright some more real punk blues... Simon and the Bar Sinisters and Popa Chubby are a couple other cats doin' it. These guys are real versed in traditional blues, but have an honest, passionate, raw take on it. Some of the Johnny Thunders acoustic shit has that sound too. Really, punk is blues so punk/blues is a redundancy. We're punks and we get the blues (or some off-white version of it) and we fuckin' play music. But what started out as a musically versatile 'punk scene' in the seventies and eighties with bands as musically varient as Black Flag, The Minute Men, The Misfits, maybe The Damned... well I wasn't there, but these bands all sound totally fuckin' different from each other. It turned into this thing where everybody sounds like the fuckin' sex pistols, and that's what people think of as 'punk music'.

So we who play contemporary music following a folk or blues tradition, we play for our people (not coca-cola). We play blues or folk music that offends traditionalists (sorry to disturb your fuckin' coffin, count), we might call it punk/blues or antifolk.

Sold one CD at an open mic tonight in Richmond to this cool fifty-something year old physicist from Boston. Tellin' him, "Yea, sometimes I worry, ya know? Getting old so fast, afraid I'll die unknown.".
He's like, "Even the 'knowns' are unknown in twenty years. Forget about being known.".
We discussed passionate underground artists from the past who no-one on this earth could name today, and how they're alive tonight in the form of me and him - WE ARE THEM.

Can you dig being part of something which spans generations and is greater than history or money or any man-made thing within life, because it's one with life itself?
