10/22/99


Writing from a tent in the yard of an old plantation-come-antique store-come blues bar-soon to be Blues University. Hobson's Preservation Company. Clarksdale, Mississippi. At the intersection of Rt.'s 61 and fourty-something, basically where the Delta Blues was born.

Monday night (October 18th), played here tonight, then went into town and sold CD's in local bars.

These old fuckin' racists tried to scare me away from one place where they ended up buying CD's AND feeding me for free.

Another place, Mingles is owned by a guy who used to be in ("old scool" rapper) Sir Mix-A-Lot's band. He wants to book me for the BET comedy show they're doing in a few weeks.

Life is grand.

Now It's Saturday afternoon at a hundred year old farm house which was empty for 20 some-odd years 'till Mike and Kim from Billygoat started living here. It's all cold ater, out-house chickens, rattle snakes and four dogs. There's a lot of stars up there, really. The nearest bar is a shack with no windaws and no neon.

Been going into bars on nights of and just walking up to people selling CD's, pretty cool actualy. I could live like this.

:-), Mike iLL
