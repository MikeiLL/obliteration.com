It's transcriptions of lectures to a bunch of hipnotists in the 1970's, based on this German hipnotist named Milton Ericson.

He also lent me a book I'm reading now called New View over Atlantis by John Michell, and eccentric British guy who's currently honorary head of the Druids and also happens to be good friends with my sister Daphne who introduced me to him ecently. The book talks about the various sciences of math and measures whos knowledge can be discerned from many pre-historic stone sites like Stone-henge, the Egyptian and South-American Pyramids, in ancient Israeli, Maltese, Roman and Greek temples. There's something called the "sacred rod". A measurement equal to about 3.5 of our feet which is actually a near-exact fraction of the circumpherence of the Earth. John Michell says that the ancients had very advanced sciences and math, and knew the size of the planet and all this shit way long ago. Even Sir Issac Newton went to Egypt to take measurements of the pyramids when trying to figure the size of our planet.
He's also talking about lay lines which can be seen defined by the placement of various ancient paths and temples, which he says come from a time when we people treated the Earth like a living being. Man. Too much to go into right now, but suffice to say this hard little mind is opening up a bit.
